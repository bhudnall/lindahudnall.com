My Story

I graduated from Arizona State University (ASU) with my Master’s Degree from the School of Social Work. After completing my graduate work, I became employed at the Employee Assistance Office (EAO) counseling faculty and staff at Arizona State University. During my years working at the EAO, I completed my certification in Life Coaching as well as becoming a True Colors facilitator.

My next stop along my journey was working as a Family Counselor at The Meadows, a world-renowned residential treatment facility that focuses on child/adult trauma and addictions. This opportunity provided me the knowledge of understanding how neurobiology and childhood attachment are fundamental in processing how childhood experiences impact our adult relationships.

Over the past few years my focus of training has been:

Psychological Approach to Couples Therapy
Stan Tatkin Psy.D

Early Attachment Wounds and Embracing The Authentic Self Creating Healthy Adult Relationships
Diane Poole Heller Ph.D

Post Induction Therapy: How Childhood Trauma Is The Origin of Developmental Immaturity
Pia Mellody
